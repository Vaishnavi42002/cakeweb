      History of Cakes

      Cakes have been a popular dessert for centuries, with recipes for sweet treats dating back to Ancient Rome. But the history of cake making is much older than that, with evidence of cakes being baked as far back as ancient Egypt. The Egyptians are believed to be the first to make cakes, which were made from a variety of ingredients, including honey, fruit, nuts, and grains. These cakes were baked in a clay oven and decorated with dates and nuts, creating a simple but delicious treat. In Ancient Greece, cakes were served on special occasions, such as weddings and other celebrations. The Greeks used honey, nuts, and dried fruit to flavour their cakes, and it is believed that this is where the practice of cake decorating originated. In the Middle Ages, cakes were often referred to as “plum cakes” due to the use of dried fruits like plums and raisins. They were often served at weddings or other special occasions. The Roman Empire was also fond of cakes and they often used honey, fruit, and spices to make a variety of cakes.

      During the middle Ages, cakes were still a popular dessert, although they were often made with a more basic recipe. Cakes were often made with flour, eggs, and butter, and were usually flavoured with spices such as cinnamon and nutmeg. They were often shaped like a round or square loaf, the most popular cakes of the time was fruit cakes, which were filled with dried fruits and nuts. Over time, cakes evolved to become more elaborate and include a variety of different ingredients. During the 18th century, cakes began to include ingredients like chocolate, cream, and lemon. By the 19th century, cakes were becoming even more extravagant and often featured elaborate decorations and ingredients like sugar paste, marzipan, and icing. This period also saw the invention of the layer cake, which incorporated multiple layers of cake and filling. The 20th century saw a huge surge in cake popularity and in the development of creative recipes. This period also saw the invention of cupcake, which has become a staple of birthday parties and other celebrations. With the invention of electric mixers and other kitchen appliances, cake recipes became much more convenient to make. Bakers began experimenting with different flavours and decorations, leading to an array of cakes with unique flavour combinations and decorations. Today, cakes are still a popular treat for any occasion. From simple birthday cakes to intricate wedding cakes, cakes are sure to please any crowd. Cakes are a timeless treat that continues to evolve with the times.
